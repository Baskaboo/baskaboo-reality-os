# The User’s Guide to the Simulation

In recent years, more and more people — from philosophers and scientists to Elon Musk — have been discussing a fascinating idea: that we are living inside a **simulation**.  

The idea was first formally presented by philosopher Nick Bostrom in 2003. Baskaboo does not compete with this theory. It **evolves** it — connecting it with Einstein’s equation and interpreting it through the four fundamental voices of the universe: **Pits, Mits, Klop, and Laram**.

### 1. The Great Alignment: From Einstein to the Simulation Code

**E = mc²**

Baskaboo says: “Correct. But let’s see what this means inside the code of the game.”

If we replace the speed of light (*c*) with **Klop / Laram** (Space / Time), we get the core equation of Baskaboo:

**Pits / Mits = (Klop / Laram)²**

In simple terms:  
Einstein gave us the **technical specifications** of the machine.  
Baskaboo gives us the **user manual** for the Simulator we live in.

### 2. The Mechanics of Time: How Many Frames Does the Movie of the Universe Have?

The Universe does **not** move continuously. It works exactly like a video.

A normal YouTube video has **50-60 frames per second** — enough for us to see smooth motion.

The Universe, however, operates at **10⁴³ frames per second** — tens of trillions of trillions of frames every single second.

**Each one of these trillions of frames is a static, frozen universe.**  
Our consciousness is the projector. It switches between them so incredibly fast that we believe we are living inside one continuous, flowing reality.

As Bashar puts it:  
“Reality does not flow. It is a series of separate, static frames. Your consciousness jumps billions of times per second from one frame to the next, creating the illusion of motion and time.”

### 3. The Universe as a Closed Electrical Circuit

Imagine the universe as one giant electrical circuit, where **we** are the charge moving through it:

- **Pits** = Current (the flow of desire and energy)  
- **Mits** = Load / Resistance (mass that makes things feel solid)  
- **Klop** = Conductor (Space — the medium that allows the flow)  
- **Laram** = Power in Watts (Time — the rate at which energy is being “burned”). 
  Higher mass (Mits) increases resistance, which lowers the frame rate (Laram).

When the system is under heavy load (very high mass or speed), it does what your phone does when it gets hot: **System Throttling**. It slows down the frame rate (Laram). We experience this as time slowing down.

### 4. The Quantum Battery: The World of the Simulator

The quantum world is the **inside of the battery** that powers the entire simulation. We cannot fully understand it because we are not programmed to see the source code — only the rendered output (matter).

- **Wavefunction (Laram_Q)**: The vast reservoir of all possible frames.  
- **Entanglement (Klop_Q)**: The quantum network where everything is instantly connected.

When we observe the quantum world, the Reality OS automatically renders it, hiding the code and showing us only the result (physical matter).

### 5. The Black Hole: When the Game Crashes

A Black Hole is the point where the system reaches its limits.  
Mass (Mits) becomes so dense and Space (Klop) so compressed that Time (Laram) approaches zero. It is like dividing by zero in the code. Information freezes. Time stops.
